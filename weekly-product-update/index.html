Business Entity Records — V1

Ready for Alignment Call Version 1.2 Author: Jeremy Harrison, PM Dated July 2, 2026

Background

The multiple phone/email/address ticket (86b91n3rj) is in late QA and is the prerequisite for this work. This PRD defines V1 of Business Entity as a standalone first-class record type. Engineering already has a V2 implementation in active development — reviewed screenshots show a fully tabbed profile page and Business selectors scaffolded into all major create flows. V1 must align with that direction so what ships now is a true subset of the V2 model, not a divergent path.

Prior ticket 86b3xum84 is superseded by this PRD.

Problem

Commercial accounts often involve multiple contacts (owner, property manager, facilities coordinator, AP contact) tied to one company. Today those people exist as disconnected Contact records with no shared organizational anchor — there's no way to represent the company itself, see all contacts and activity under it, or attach a deal or proposal to the business rather than one individual.

Goal — Ship a Business Entity record type in V1 that:

  • Exists as its own standalone record (not a field on Contact)
  • Can be created from the same entry points the V2 build is targeting
  • Has its own profile page with a tabbed activity view matching the V2 structure
  • Allows contacts to be associated to it
  • Surfaces on the Contact list with a Business column and filter
  • Lays a clean foundation for the full V2 model without requiring re-architecture

What V1 Is Not

  • Not a full V2 implementation — richer pipeline integration and deeper invoice/proposal anchoring come later
  • Not a replacement for Contact as the communications anchor — Drips, SMS, and emails still originate from Contact records
  • No org hierarchy, multi-location entities, or franchise structures
  • No bulk reassignment of existing contacts to a Business Entity
  • No Business Entity-level drip automation or blast targeting
  • Customer Portal: only what already appears on the Proposal view — no entity profile surface on the portal

Core Concepts

Business Entity: a named record representing a company or organization, with its own profile page and tabbed activity view, existing independently of any Contact record.

Legal Name vs. DBA: the entity stores a Legal Name (registered name) and an optional DBA. The DBA is the short display label used throughout the UI (e.g. the header abbreviation). A toggle controls whether the DBA or Legal Name appears on customer-facing documents.

Associated Contact: a Contact linked to one Business Entity at most. Visible on both the Contact record and the Business Entity profile.

Primary Contact: one associated contact designated as primary for the entity, reachable from the profile header via "View Primary Contact," and used as the default for communication initiated from the entity.

Navigation

A "Businesses" item is added to the Sales section of the left nav, below Contacts — consistent with the V2 dev build.

Business Entity Profile Fields

Required: Legal Name

Optional:

  • DBA (Doing Business As)
  • Email (multiple supported, one Primary — same pattern as Contact per 86b91n3rj)
  • Phone (multiple supported, one Primary, with type label: Mobile, Office, etc.)
  • Industry
  • Address (structured: Street, City, State, ZIP)
  • Billing Address (separate from Address; defaults to Address if not specified)
  • Display DBA on documents (toggle: Enabled / Disabled)

Profile Page: Header Actions

Consistent with the V2 build: Call Business (uses Primary Phone), Edit Business, Archive, Delete, View Primary Contact.

Archive and Delete are separate actions — archiving preserves the record and its history; deleting removes it.

⚠️ Assumption: Delete is blocked if the entity has open Deals, Jobs, or Invoices — needs confirmation from Jason before ticketing.

Profile Page: Tab Structure

The V2 build establishes a tabbed profile layout that V1 must reuse. Each tab is scoped to data associated with the entity.

Creation Entry Points

The V2 dev team is already building the Business / Individual Customer selector into these flows. V1 must produce a real Business Entity record from each — not a throwaway inline entry.

  • Businesses nav tab — a "New Business" action on the Businesses list page creates a standalone entity directly
  • New Lead — Step 2 presents Individual Customer / Business, then Existing / New. Business + New collects Business Name and Address inline; a real Business Entity record is created when the lead is saved
  • New Appointment — Step 2 collects Business Name and Primary Contact; a Business Entity record is created on save for the New path
  • New Proposal — same Business / Individual selector; final summary step confirms "Create a new business record"; the entity is created on proposal save
  • Create Invoice — first step asks "Who is this invoice for?" (Individual Contact or Business); Business + New creates an entity on save

In all Existing paths, the user searches for and selects an already-created Business Entity — no duplicate entity is created.

Contact List Integration

A Business column displays the entity abbreviation (DBA if set, otherwise a shortened Legal Name) with an icon for each contact that has an association. A Business filter dropdown allows filtering the Contact list by entity. Both are already scaffolded in the V2 build — V1 wires them to real data.

Association Rules

  • A Contact can be associated to one Business Entity at a time
  • The association can be set from the Contact record or from the Business Entity profile
  • The association can be removed at any time — the Contact returns to unassociated status
  • If a Business Entity is deleted, all associated Contacts return to unassociated status; Contact records are not deleted

Communications Impact

All communications (Drip, SMS, email, blast) continue to originate from Contact records. The Business Entity does not become a sender, recipient designator, or trigger source in V1 — no communications impact to existing behavior.

Dependency

86b91n3rj (Multiple Phone Numbers, Emails, Addresses) must reach production before Business Entity development begins.

V2 Alignment Notes

  • The V2 dev build reviewed in screenshots defines the intended end state — V1 must not contradict it
  • The tabbed profile structure shown in the V2 build is the target layout; V1 ships this structure, and individual tabs may have simplified or deferred content where backend work is unresolved
  • The Business / Individual Customer 2x2 selector in create flows must produce real entity records in V1
  • The "Businesses" nav item and Contact list Business column are already scaffolded — V1 wires them to real data
  • Translation keys visible in some V2 screens (e.g. newOpportunity.form.newBusinessInfo) indicate those strings are in progress — V1 should not depend on those flows being finalized
  • Legal Name / DBA / Industry / Display DBA on documents / Billing Address are all present in the V2 profile build and must be included in V1
