Contact Email Unsubscribe Toggles

A plain-language guide to the three opt-out toggles on the Customer Portal unsubscribe page. Use this to explain toggle behavior to customers and to verify whether a contact is correctly opted in or out.

Toggle 1 of 3
Unsubscribe from marketing emails
OptOutOfNewsletters

Turning this on stops broadcast-style marketing messages sent to groups of contacts, like newsletters or email blasts. It has no effect on anything related to a specific job or deal.

  • ✕ Newsletters
  • ✕ Email blast campaigns
  • ✓ Drip sequences still send
  • ✓ Proposals, invoices, payment receipts still send
  • ✓ Appointment reminders still send
  • ✓ Password resets and security emails still send
Toggle 2 of 3
Unsubscribe from job related messages
OptOutOfDrips ⚠ Label is misleading

Despite being called "job related messages," this toggle only stops automated drip sequences — the lifecycle follow-up emails that fire as a deal moves through pipeline stages. It does not block proposals, invoices, or reminders.

  • ✕ Automated drip sequence emails
  • ✓ Proposals still send
  • ✓ Invoices still send
  • ✓ Appointment confirmations and reminders still send
  • ✓ Payment notifications still send
  • ✓ Password resets still send
Common CS scenario: A customer says "I unsubscribed from job messages but I'm still getting my proposal." That is correct behavior — this toggle only stops drips, not transactional documents. Only Toggle 3 stops those.
Toggle 3 of 3
Unsubscribe from all emails
OptOutOfAllEmails ⚠ Master kill-switch

This is the master kill-switch. When turned on, it overrides the other two toggles and stops everything customer-facing — including drips, newsletters, proposals, invoices, and appointment reminders.

  • ✕ Drip sequences
  • ✕ Newsletters and blasts
  • ✕ Proposals
  • ✕ Invoices
  • ✕ Appointment confirmations and reminders
  • ✕ Payment notifications
  • ✓ Password resets always send (no opt-out, by design)
  • ✓ Account security alerts always send
Important: If a contact is not receiving proposals or invoices, check this toggle first. When it is on, no documents will reach them regardless of Toggle 1 or Toggle 2.
How They Work Together
Toggles 1 and 2 are independent — each only affects its own category. Toggle 3 overrides both. When Toggle 3 is on, Toggles 1 and 2 are irrelevant — nothing customer-facing sends.

CS Troubleshooting Checklist

When a contact reports not receiving emails, work through these steps in order.

  1. 1
    Check Toggle 3 (Unsubscribe from all emails) first. If it is on, nothing customer-facing will send. This is the most common cause of missing proposals and invoices.
  2. 2
    If the contact says they are missing drip sequence emails, check Toggle 2. If it is on, drips are blocked — but proposals and invoices are not affected.
  3. 3
    If the contact says they are missing newsletters or blast campaigns, check Toggle 1. If it is on, broadcast marketing is blocked — drips and job documents are not affected.
  4. 4
    If all three toggles are off and the contact still is not receiving emails, the issue is elsewhere (spam filtering, incorrect email address, Postmark delivery failure). Escalate accordingly.
  5. 5
    Password resets can never be blocked by any toggle. If a contact is not receiving a reset email, the cause is outside these settings.
